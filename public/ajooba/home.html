The Internet: Introduction

According to The Tech Terms Dictionary, the internet can be defined as a globally connected network system which uses TCP/IP for data transmission via various types of media. The internet is a network of global exchanges; which includes private, public, business, academic and government networks, connected by guided, wireless and fiber-optic technologies.

Often, the terms World Wide Web and the internet are used interchangeably, however, it is important to note that they are not the same thing. The internet refers to the global communication system, including hardware and infrastructure, while the web is one of the services communicated over the internet. [1]

The Internet: Past

The internet was originated by the US government in the 1960’s when they began building a computer network known as ARPANET. The U.S. National Science Foundation (NSF) commissioned the development of a university network backbone called NSFNET.

New networks that operated by commercial ISPs (Internet Service Providers) replaced this system in 1995. The internet was brought to the public on a larger scale at around this time. [2]

Internet Timeline

A summarized timeline of key years in the history of the internet have been provided below:

  • 1962: J. C. R. Licklider proposed a global network
  • 1969: ARPANET established
  • 1971: First email message
  • 1974: TCP was born
  • 1984: DNS was born
  • 1985: First domain name registered
  • 1986: Cisco launched its first router
  • 1990: HTML was introduced
  • 1991: World's first website
  • 1994: Amazon was founded
  • 1997: IEEE community created the 802.11 Standard
  • 1998: IPv6 was introduced
  • 1998: 3G networks were introduced
  • 1998: Google search engine was founded
  • 1999: Internet of Things concept was introduced
  • 2008: 4G networks were released
  • 2013: Google began pilot experiments of Google Loon Projects [3]

The Internet: Future

The internet is something that many of us take for granted as it is today. We have almost forgotten how it used to be–slow, time-consuming, with less content. And anybody who is old enough is struggling to remember what it was like before we had the internet at all. The whole thing made quite a breakthrough, one that we may not have all noticed gradually. But that's as good as it gets? Is there anything else to come from the internet? Technology experts believe this and predict a very exciting internet future and other internet technology future that can lead us to the future of multifunction.

Forecasts of the future of the internet cover a wide range, as you might guess, but in most sources and forecasts, there are a few concepts that stand out [5] :

We’ve been connecting to the Internet in various ways, from the minutes-long process of dialing up to the seconds-long process of entering a Wi-Fi password once for a given location. Eventually, connectivity will be constant and streamlined to the point where no individual “connection” is really necessary. Universal Internet is slowly becoming a reality, and overlap between systems may establish a layer of redundancy that prevents worries of service outages or bad connections. [5]

Mobile devices will allow us to access the Internet in the real world, but next-gen devices will either project or embed the Internet into the real world through a form of augmented reality. Although Augmented Reality has seen some disappointing fits and starts, its close cousin, virtual reality, is starting to see significant growth in the wake of the release of Oculus Rift, HTC Vive, and the new PlayStation VR. A few years ago, the world wasn't prepared for AR, but it will be a few years from now.[5]
